================================================================================
IMPLICATIONS FOR USERS
================================================================================

1. FIRST-TIME SUPPLIERS pay a ~80,680 gas premium
   - This is unavoidable due to EVM storage pricing
   - Represents ~24.8% higher cost

2. REPEAT SUPPLIERS benefit from warm storage
   - All subsequent supplies are cheaper
   - The more you supply, the more you save (amortized)

3. OPTIMIZATION STRATEGIES:
   - Batch multiple small supplies into one larger supply
   - First supply should be meaningful (amortize cold cost)
   - Consider gas prices when timing first supply

4. CROSS-TOKEN CONSIDERATIONS:
   - Each token has separate user mappings
   - First supply of AVAX is cold, even if user supplied USDC before
   - Pool-level storage (like rate limits) may be warm across tokens

================================================================================

SSTORE Costs:
  - Cold slot (0 -> non-zero):     22,100 gas
  - Warm slot (non-zero -> diff):   2,900 gas
  - Warm slot (non-zero -> zero):   2,900 gas + 4,800 refund
  - No-op (value unchanged):          100 gas

SLOAD Costs:
  - Cold slot (first access):       2,100 gas
  - Warm slot (subsequent):           100 gas

  - Not used in these transactions

================================================================================
CONCLUSION
================================================================================

The 80,680 gas difference between cold and warm supply is explained by:

  SSTORE savings (3 user slots x 19,200):     57,600 gas
  _largeHolders increment savings:              2,900 gas
  SLOAD savings (reads warm vs cold):         ~10,000 gas
  Wrapper + misc cold storage:                ~10,180 gas
                                             ----------
  Total:                                      ~80,680 gas

The EVM's storage pricing model heavily penalizes first-time interactions,
but rewards repeat usage. This is by design to:
  - Discourage state bloat
  - Amortize the cost of permanent storage
  - Incentivize efficient contract design
