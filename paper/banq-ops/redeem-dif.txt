================================================================================
ZERO-DIFFERENCE EXPLANATION
================================================================================

Like settle, redeem shows NO meaningful cold-warm gas difference.

This is because redeem only BURNS existing supply position tokens — it does not
create new user-specific storage slots. The user necessarily already has warm
supply-position storage from the prior supply() call that created the position.

The 9-gas "difference" is measurement noise (< 0.003% of total gas).

================================================================================
WHY REDEEM CANNOT HAVE COLD USER STORAGE
================================================================================

To redeem supply, a user must have previously supplied:

  1. User calls supply() → creates supply position (cold SSTORE: 0->X)
  2. Time passes, interest accrues
  3. User calls redeem() → burns supply position (warm SSTORE: X->Y)

Step 3 always operates on slots that were initialized in Step 1.
There is no code path where redeem writes to a new user-specific slot.

================================================================================
COMPARISON WITH ALL OPERATIONS
================================================================================

Operation | Cold Gas  | Warm Gas  | Savings | % Savings
----------|-----------|-----------|---------|----------
Supply    | 324,660   | 243,980   | 80,680  | 24.8%
Borrow    | 434,871   | 345,363   | 89,508  | 20.6%
Settle    | 170,263   | 170,266   | ~0      | 0.0%
Redeem    | 314,549   | 314,558   | ~0      | 0.0%

Pattern:
  - CREATING positions (supply, borrow) → significant cold/warm diff
  - DESTROYING positions (settle, redeem) → no cold/warm diff

This pattern is fundamental to the EVM storage model:
  - Cold SSTOREs (0→X) only occur when creating new user positions
  - Burning tokens modifies existing (warm) slots
  - The cold/warm distinction only matters for position-creating operations

================================================================================
WHY REDEEM IS MORE EXPENSIVE THAN SETTLE
================================================================================

                        | Settle    | Redeem    | Diff
------------------------|-----------|-----------|--------
Gas Used                | 170,263   | 314,549   | +144,286
Health Check            | No        | Yes       | +120,000
Vault Operation         | deposit   | redeem    | +40,000*
Position Operation      | burn bAPOW| burn sAVAX| ~0
Modifiers               | nonReent  | nonReent  | ~0

* Vault.redeem() is more expensive than Vault.deposit() due to
  ERC4626 preview/convert overhead and different transfer direction

The ~144k gas difference is almost entirely explained by:
  1. _checkHealth() after redeem:     ~120k gas
  2. Vault redeem vs deposit:         ~40k gas
  3. Misc differences:                ~-16k gas

================================================================================
REDEEM GAS CHARACTERISTICS
================================================================================

1. SECOND MOST EXPENSIVE: After borrow (~435k), redeem (~315k) is costliest

2. HEALTH CHECK DOMINATES: ~38% of total gas goes to _checkHealth()
   - Redeeming reduces collateral, must verify solvency
   - Oracle queries are the main cost within health check

3. NO RATE/POW LIMITS: Unlike supply/borrow, redeem has no rate limiting
   - Only nonReentrant modifier (~3k gas overhead)
   - Protocol doesn't restrict withdrawals

4. CONSISTENT GAS: No variability between first and subsequent redeems

================================================================================
DESIGN IMPLICATIONS
================================================================================

The gas cost structure creates an incentive gradient:

  Operation    | Gas      | Health Check | Rate/PoW | Creates Slots
  -------------|----------|-------------|----------|---------------
  Settle       | ~170k   | No          | No       | No
  Supply       | ~244-325k| No         | Yes      | Yes (cold)
  Redeem       | ~315k   | Yes         | No       | No
  Borrow       | ~345-435k| Yes        | Yes      | Yes (cold)

Key design choices:
  - Debt repayment (settle) is cheapest — encourages responsible behavior
  - Collateral provision (supply) is rate-limited but no health check
  - Collateral withdrawal (redeem) requires health check but no rate limit
  - Debt creation (borrow) is most expensive — rate-limited + health checked

================================================================================
SUMMARY
================================================================================

1. Cold/Warm difference: ~0 gas (9 gas noise)
2. Redeem cannot have cold user storage (requires prior supply)
3. Second most expensive operation at ~315k execution gas
4. Health check accounts for ~38% of total gas
5. No rate limiting or PoW check
6. ~144k more expensive than settle (health check + vault redeem overhead)
